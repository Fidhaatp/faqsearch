General Questions

What health screenings do I need?
Recommended screenings vary by age, gender, and medical history. Most adults should get blood pressure checks, cholesterol tests, and diabetes screenings annually. Consult your physician for personalized recommendations.
How do I prepare for a blood test?
Typically requires 8-12 hours fasting (water allowed). Avoid strenuous exercise 24 hours before. Inform your doctor about any medications. Wear loose clothing for easy arm access.

Preparation & Guidelines

Can I drink water before screening?
Yes, plain water is allowed. Avoid other beverages like coffee, tea, or juice. Stay hydrated but don't overconsume - 1-2 glasses is sufficient.
Should I stop medications before tests?
Never stop prescribed medications without consulting your doctor. Some supplements/vitamins might need pausing - inform our staff about all substances you're taking.

Testing & Results

How long for test results?
Most results within 24-48 hours. Complex tests (like cancer markers) may take 3-5 days. You'll receive results via secure portal with doctor's interpretation.
What if my results are abnormal?
Don't panic - many factors can affect results. Our medical team will contact you to discuss findings and next steps, which may include follow-up tests or specialist referrals.

Privacy & Safety

Is my health data confidential?
Absolutely. We follow HIPAA regulations with encrypted data storage. Results are only shared with authorized personnel and your designated physician.
What COVID safety measures are in place?
All staff wear PPE, rooms sanitized between patients, HEPA air filtration, and optional contactless check-in. We follow CDC guidelines for healthcare facilities.
